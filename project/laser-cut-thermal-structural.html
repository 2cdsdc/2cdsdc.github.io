Coupled Transient Thermal & Structural Analysis of Laser Cutting

A detailed CAE study performed in ANSYS 2024 R1 to evaluate the thermal and mechanical response of a steel component subjected to laser cutting over time using coupled transient simulation.

Simulation Overview

This project analyzes the effect of laser cutting on a structural steel component by coupling transient thermal results with structural response. Temperature-dependent strains and nonlinear effects were considered to capture realistic mechanical behavior during the cutting process.

Key Technical Highlights

  • Material: Structural Steel (nonlinear + thermal strain enabled)
  • Heat Input: Volumetric temperature mapping
  • Simulation Time: 10 seconds
  • Maximum Temperature: 165.14 °C
  • Average Temperature: ~102 °C
  • Maximum Equivalent Stress: 121.3 MPa
  • Average Stress: ~58.7 MPa
  • Mesh: ~7,982 nodes | 1,196 elements
  • Contacts: 29 bonded face-to-face
  • Supports: Frictionless constraints
  • Solver: Mechanical APDL (large deflection enabled)
